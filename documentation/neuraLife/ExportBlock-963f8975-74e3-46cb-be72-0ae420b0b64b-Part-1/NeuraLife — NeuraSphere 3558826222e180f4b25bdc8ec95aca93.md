## Purpose & Scope

NeuraSphere is the social layer of the entire NeuraLife community. It is not a school feature — it is a platform feature. Every student enrolled in any NeuraLife-registered school who meets the age gate is part of one interconnected network from day one.

**What NeuraSphere is:**

- A verified achievement-sharing platform where badges are earned through real SmartPad learning
- A peer learning community where students share study tips, doubts, and progress
- A professional-style identity layer that follows the student across schools and into alumni life
- An inter-school community connecting students across AP and Telangana by interest and achievement

**What NeuraSphere is not:**

- An entertainment platform (no viral content, no engagement maximisation algorithm)
- An anonymous network (real names only, verified by school principal)
- A private channel (parents see everything)
- A distraction (no infinite scroll, no push notification addiction mechanics)

**Where NeuraSphere lives:**
NeuraSphere is part of the **Parent & Student App** (Student Login mode). It is not a separate app. When a student logs in to the Parent & Student App using their NeuraID + PIN, their band determines whether NeuraSphere is accessible and at what level.

> **The trust proposition:** NeuraSphere is the only student social network in India where every profile is verified by a school principal, every achievement is earned through AI-assessed learning, every post is moderated before publishing, and every parent has full visibility. This is what makes principals accept it and parents trust it.

## Age Gate — Four Bands

Access to NeuraSphere is controlled by the student's **band**, computed from their current class year. The principal cannot override the age gate.

| Band | Classes | Ages | NeuraSphere access |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Foundation** | 1–3 | 6–9 | ❌ No access. Not shown in app. All recognition flows through parent view only. |
| **Elementary** | 4–6 | 9–12 | ❌ No access. Stickers, achievements, and badges visible to parent only. |
| **Middle** | 7–8 | 12–14 | ✅ **Limited access.** See dedicated section below. |
| **Secondary** | 9–10 | 14–16 | ✅ **Full access.** All features available. |

**Why Classes 4–6 have no access:** Social network participation requires digital literacy and emotional maturity that develops at the Middle band (12+). Elementary band students benefit from a distraction-free environment. Their achievement recognition goes directly to the parent — stickers, badges, celebrations — without an inter-school social layer.

**Why Classes 7–8 are limited, not excluded:** Middle band students benefit from community belonging and cross-school academic connections. But direct posting and chat carry risks at this age that outweigh the benefits. Limited access builds good social media habits before full access at Class 9.

## The Four Pillars

### Pillar 1 — Verified Identity

Every NeuraSphere profile is automatically created and populated from school enrollment data. Students cannot create profiles manually.

**Profile fields (all pulled from NeuraID):**

- Real full name (from enrollment — no aliases, no usernames)
- Current school name, class, section (verified by principal)
- NeuraID number (tappable → opens Community ID Card with QR code)
- Learning Journey timeline (all schools attended on NeuraLife)
- Account type: Public or Private (student chooses, Class 9+ only)
- Band-specific access label: "Limited Access" (Class 7–8) or full profile (Class 9–10)
- "Verified by NeuraLife Community" seal

**What the student can customise (Secondary band):**

- Profile photo (AI-moderated before display)
- Short bio (max 100 characters, AI-moderated)
- Account privacy (Public or Private)
- Which badges to pin (max 3 pinned)
- Leaderboard visibility (opt-in, default: off)

**What cannot be changed (any band):**

- Name (from enrollment)
- School, class, section (from school admin)
- NeuraID (permanent)
- Verification status (automatic)
- Band / access level (from class year)

### Pillar 3 — Post Types

**Type 1 — Achievement posts (system-generated)**

Generated when a badge is earned. For Middle band: auto-generated text only (system-authored). For Secondary band: student can add a personal note.

```
System-generated text: "Ravi Kumar just mastered Quadratic Equations! 🎉"
Secondary band adds: "Finally! Took me 7 sessions but persistence pays off."

v1: Triggered from mobile NeuraSphere (student taps "Share this achievement")
v2: Triggered directly from SmartPad after achievement animation
```

- Content: Badge name, subject, optional student note
- Media: Achievement card graphic (no photo)
- Moderation: Auto-moderated — achievement posts are always clean, no human review needed
- Published: Within 60 seconds of submission

**Type 2 — Manual posts (Secondary band only, from mobile app)**

Student composes from Parent & Student App (Student Login, Class 9–10).

- Subtypes: Text only / Photo + caption / Study tip
- Text limit: 300 characters
- Photo: 1 image per post, max 5MB, AI-moderated for inappropriate imagery
- Tags: Subject tags (#Mathematics, #Telugu), class tags (#Class10), custom hashtags
- Full moderation flow before publishing
- Middle band (7–8): ❌ Cannot write manual posts

**Type 3 — Doubt posts (Secondary band, v2)**

A specific post subtype for academic questions:

- Tagged as "Doubt" — visually distinct in feed
- Subject and topic tagged automatically
- Other students can mark answers as "Helpful" → triggers "Helpful Peer" badge
- Teacher App: class teacher sees doubt posts from their students in a separate tab
- v2 feature — requires community size to be useful

**Type 4 — SmartPad contextual posts (v2 AOSP only)**

Available when v2 AOSP SmartPad is deployed. A share button appears in specific contexts:

- Chapter completed successfully
- Streak milestone hit (7, 30, term)
- Problem solved after multiple failed attempts
- Writing practice session with improved clarity score

Text only (no image upload from SmartPad). Student adds note (max 150 characters). Published after AI moderation. **Not available in v1 kiosk app.**

### Pillar 4 — Feed Algorithm

Priority-ordered, chronological within each tier. **No engagement maximisation. No infinite scroll.**

```
Tier 1: Same class, same school
  → Posts from classmates in the same section
  → Shown first, chronologically
  → All posts visible regardless of follow status

Tier 2: Same school, different class
  → Posts from schoolmates
  → Visible if student follows them (public) or mutual follow (private)

Tier 3: Interest-matched, different schools
  → Students from other NeuraLife schools with overlapping interest tags
  → Interest tags derived from: strongest mastery subjects, recently completed
    chapters, earned badges (auto-derived, no manual selection)
  → Shown if student follows them

Tier 4: Trending across NeuraLife
  → High-engagement posts across full platform
  → Filtered for same class year ±1 (age-appropriate)
  → Maximum 3 trending posts per feed refresh

Tier 5 (v2): Trending in your school
  → Horizontally scrollable strip above main feed
  → Top 3 most-liked posts from your school in last 24 hours
  → Creates school pride without disrupting main feed
```

**Anti-addiction design:**

- Feed loads in pages of 10 posts — no infinite scroll
- No "you're all caught up — here's more content" prompt
- No engagement streaks or daily login rewards (streaks are SmartPad learning streaks, not app-open streaks)
- NeuraSphere push notifications: max 3 per day per student
- No aggressive "people you may know" suggestions

## Account Privacy (Secondary Band Only)

Middle band (7–8): Privacy settings not available — profiles are always visible to class members. Posts do not exist to be private.

Secondary band (9–10): Students choose Public or Private on their profile. Default: Public.

## Engagement System (Secondary Band)

**Allowed interactions on every post:**

- ❤️ **Like** — tap to like, tap again to unlike
- 🎉 **Celebrate** — for achievement posts specifically (different from Like)
- 💡 **Helpful** — for study tips and doubt posts
- 💬 **Comment** — text only, max 150 characters, AI-moderated before visible
- 🔗 **Share** — share post to your Learning Circle feed (not external platforms)
- 🔖 **Save** — save post to personal collection (visible only to self)

**Three reactions (not one):** Like, Celebrate, Helpful. Each has a distinct use case. Keeps engagement meaningful without complexity. Reaction counts displayed separately on posts.

**Comment rules:**

- AI-moderated before visible to others (same pipeline as posts)
- Approved: visible within 5 seconds
- Flagged: held for teacher review
- Comment author cannot delete their own comment — teacher/principal only
- No reply-to-comment threading in v1 (flat comment list). Threaded replies in v2.

**What is not allowed:**

- Sharing posts outside NeuraLife (no "copy link," no WhatsApp share, no external platform share)
- Tagging other students by name in posts or comments
- Posting personal contact information (phone, address, email, Aadhaar)
- Emoji-only posts (must contain meaningful text)

**Middle Band engagement:** Like and Save only. No commenting. No sharing. No reactions other than ❤️.

## Moderation Architecture

### AI Moderation Pipeline

Every post and comment passes through this pipeline before becoming visible.

**Step 1 — Content extraction (< 100ms)**

- Extract text from post/comment
- If image: run image content classification (nudity, violence, personal documents)
- Identify language: English, Telugu, or mixed (Tenglish)

**Step 2 — Classification (< 2 seconds)**

Using Claude API for text moderation — handles Telugu, English, and mixed-language content reliably.

| Category | Examples | Action |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Profanity (Telugu + English) | Abusive words in either language | Auto-reject if confidence ≥ 0.90 |
| Bullying | Targeting a named student negatively | Auto-reject if confidence ≥ 0.90 |
| Personal information | Phone numbers, addresses, Aadhaar | Auto-reject if confidence ≥ 0.90 |
| Inappropriate imagery | Nudity, violence, unrelated content | Auto-reject if confidence ≥ 0.90 |
| Spam | Repetitive content, promotional links | Auto-reject if confidence ≥ 0.90 |
| Uncertain/borderline | Ambiguous language, context-dependent | Hold for human review |

**Step 3 — Decision**

```
Confidence ≥ 0.90 CLEAN       → Publish immediately. "AI verified ✓" label.
Confidence 0.60–0.89          → Hold. Send to class teacher review queue.
Confidence ≥ 0.90 HARMFUL     → Auto-reject. Student notified with category.
```

**Step 4 — Student notification on rejection**

- "Your post did not meet NeuraLife Community Guidelines."
- Category shown: "Inappropriate language" / "Personal information detected" / "Content not suitable"
- No specific explanation of what triggered it (avoids gaming the filter)
- Student can appeal once — goes directly to principal queue

### Human Review Escalation

```
Class teacher notified in Teacher App (posts held for review):
  → Teacher has 24 hours to review
  → Actions: Approve → post goes live | Reject with reason | Escalate to principal

If teacher takes no action in 24 hours → Principal queue
  → Principal has 24 hours
  → Actions: Approve | Reject | Escalate to NeuraLife moderation team

If principal takes no action in 24 hours → NeuraLife moderation team
  → Final decision made
  → Fed back as training signal for moderation model
```

### Repeat Violation Policy

| Violations | Action |
| --- | --- |
| 1st auto-reject | Student notified, no further action |
| 2nd auto-reject | Class teacher notified |
| 3rd auto-reject | Account flagged — principal review required before next post |
| 4th+ | Posting suspended 7 days — parent notified via FCM push |

### Moderation Model Improvement

Every human review decision logged as a training signal. Quarterly retraining of the Claude-based moderation prompt on accumulated human decisions. Target: reduce uncertain-confidence posts held for human review to < 5% of all posts within 6 months.

| --- | --- |

| Comments on child's posts | ✅ Full content |

## Transfer Behaviour

When a student transfers schools, their NeuraSphere history is preserved:

**Old posts:** Permanently show the school where they were posted, with a small "Alumni · Sunrise School" label beneath the post. This preserves authenticity of the learning journey.

**New posts:** Show the new school badge: "Vikas High School · Class 10-A."

**Achievements:** All badges earned at the old school remain on their profile permanently. Visible with school context shown.

**Learning Circle:** All connections remain. Cross-school connections become even more meaningful — the student now knows people from multiple schools.

## Feed Personalisation — Interest Tag Derivation

Interest tags derived automatically from learning behaviour. No manual tag selection.

```python
def derive_interest_tags(neura_id):
  mastery_map = fetch_mastery_map(neura_id)
  tags = []

  # Top 2 strongest subjects (where mastery is highest)
  top_subjects = mastery_map.subjects.sorted_by(mastery_pct).top(2)
  tags.extend([s.subject for s in top_subjects])

  # Recently completed chapters (last 14 days)
  recent_chapters = fetch_recent_completions(neura_id, days=14)
  tags.extend([c.topic_tag for c in recent_chapters])

  # Earned badges (subject context)
  badges = fetch_badges(neura_id)
  tags.extend([b.subject_tag for b in badges if b.subject_tag])

  return deduplicate(tags)[:5]  # max 5 interest tags

# Refreshed weekly — feeds Tier 3 (interest-matched inter-school) content
```

## Confirmed Decisions

| Decision | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| NeuraSphere is platform-wide from day one | Not per-school, not v3. Every student on any NeuraLife school who meets the age gate is in the same community. Feed is prioritised by proximity (Tier 1–4) but the network is one. |
| Four-band age gate (not two-tier) | Foundation (1–3): none. Elementary (4–6): none. Middle (7–8): limited. Secondary (9–10): full. Previous two-tier (1–6 hidden, 7–12 full) was wrong. |
| Middle band: achievements + circles only, no posting, no chat | Social exposure before full posting rights builds healthy social media habits. At Class 9, students have used the feed for 2 years — responsible by design. |
| SmartPad posts: v2 AOSP only | v1 kiosk app has no NeuraSphere layer. Achievement prompt on SmartPad is v2. v1: student shares achievements from mobile. |
| Writing badges: v2 (WSS-1 required) | WSS-1 is a v2 Edge AI model. Badges that depend on WSS-1 data cannot be awarded in v1. All other badges are v1. |
| Three reactions (Like / Celebrate / Helpful) | One reaction type (like only) is too blunt. Three types make engagement meaningful: Celebrate for achievements, Helpful for study tips, Like for general appreciation. |
| Leaderboard: class-level only, opt-in, default off | School-wide or platform-wide leaderboard creates unhealthy comparison. Class-level leaderboard in a small group (40 students) creates healthy motivation. Opt-in prevents anxiety for students who do not want to be ranked. |
| Old posts show old school with "Alumni" label | Preserves authenticity. A post from Class 8 at Sunrise School should show that context — not pretend it was from the current school. |
| No external search engine indexing — ever | Students are minors. All NeuraSphere content is behind login, always. No exceptions for "public" profiles. |
| Parent sees everything — no exceptions | This is the feature that makes principals say yes. Without it, NeuraSphere cannot be deployed in schools. |
| No sharing to external platforms | No "copy link," no WhatsApp share, no Instagram share. The community stays inside NeuraLife. |
| Posts cannot be deleted by students | Only teachers and principals can delete posts. Prevents gaming of the moderation system (post something, delete before review). |
